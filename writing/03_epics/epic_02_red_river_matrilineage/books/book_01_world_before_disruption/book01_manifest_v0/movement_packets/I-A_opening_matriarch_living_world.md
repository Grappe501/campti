# I-A — Opening matriarch / establish living world

Canon status: PROPOSED

## Movement Purpose

Establish the **living** Native / Red River world through the opening matriarch’s **authority, labor, and relationships**—so the reader feels the place is **real, beautiful, worth defending**, and ordered by **women’s intelligence and kinship logic** without expository lecture.

## Primary Woman / Matriarchal Figure

**Opening matriarch** (Third I center). Name, age, exact title: **OPEN** / PROPOSED; align with Chitimacha anchor and `spine_packet/historical_worldbuilding/` queues.

## Her Power In This Movement

- **Bodily river knowledge:** current, mud, temperature, fish/season cues as workplace read (not mystic monologue).
- **Kinship accounting:** who belongs to which hearth, who owes help, who speaks when—shown through **distribution of tasks** and **who waits**.
- **Spatial jurisdiction:** where children may go, where visitors halt, where food and trade goods are received.
- **Diplomatic pacing:** she can slow a moment so the community reads a stranger or a new object **together**—power as tempo, not speech volume.

## What She Wants

- Tomorrow predictable for **children and hearths** she is responsible for.
- **Respect for protocol** (guests, kin, trade) so alliances do not fracture from haste or insult.
- Her **pattern of power** to remain teachable—daughter generation watching and stepping in where appropriate.

## What She Fears

- **Haste** from inside (young warriors, proud men) or outside (traders, colonial presence) that breaks protocol.
- **Misread of a small trade shift** as “nothing” when it is the first loosening of obligation.
- **Loss of memory**—if younger women stop carrying names and obligations, the village becomes loud but fragile.

## What She Builds

- A **felt sense of order**: the village as a place where work, children, and river belong to one intelligible day.
- **Trust** in her judgment without her becoming the only voice—other women’s competence visible in parallel.
- **First attachment** for the reader: love rooted in **competence + care + humor** (see `../../prose_doctrine/character_depth_doctrine.md`).

## What She Transfers Forward

- The **pattern** matriarchal authority uses (river + kinship + household + tempo).
- **Seeds** for I-B / I-C: a leadership test already **implied** by how she moves through the day.
- **Daughter / mother generation** positioned as **next carrier** of the pattern (foreground or background—OPEN).

## Visible Story Movement

- Day opens; **work begins**; children move; the matriarch’s body and decisions organize space.
- Reader sees **village through her choices**: where she looks, what she corrects lightly, what she refuses to rush.
- **Beauty** emerges from **specific labor + river + food + sound**—worth protecting because it is **shared life**, not postcard.

## Hidden Historical Pressure

- **Trade-route hints:** unfamiliar goods, metal, cloth, language fragment—arrive inside **ordinary hospitality or labor** (`../red_river_pressure_manifest.md`).
- **French / colonial world** enters as **pressure on time and obligation**, not as sermon or villain track.
- **Alliance strain** possible as a **paused glance** between women after a visitor leaves—no repeated warnings.

## Binding / Union Thread

- **Not** the granddaughter’s binding yet—plant **binding logic as community practice**: how matches are talked about, deferred, prepared, joked about among women.
- Show **marriage/binding as household strategy** in small gestures (who sits where, what gift means).

## Destined Union Convergence

- Geography: **river + portage + meeting places** make certain encounters recurrent; not fate, **pattern**.
- Kinship: obligations create **who must be in the same space** at the same time.
- Trade: goods and guests **force** introductions and tests of protocol—future unions emerge from **repeated proximity + need**, not coincidence.

## Red River / Trade Route Function

- River = **food, travel, news, danger, boundary**—always **doing work in scene** (`../../prose_doctrine/nature_and_setting_doctrine.md`).
- Trade = **objects and bodies** crossing space; women’s labor integrates outside goods into **household meaning**.

## Village / Household Life Function

- Multiple hearths visible; **ensemble** competence; children’s tasks prove scale.
- Village not backdrop: **paths, sounds, smells, who owns which chore** matter for attachment (`../reader_attachment_manifest.md`).

## Nature / Setting Function

- Track sensory minimums per doctrine: **light, water, bank, weather, food prep, insects/birds as timekeepers**, children’s height-of-eye.
- Nature as **power**: flood risk, fog, heat—whatever research locks—should shape **plans for the day**, not decorate.
