# Historical Density Doctrine

Canon status: PROPOSED

## Purpose

Historical detail should make the world feel real without becoming a lecture.

## Detail Types To Build

- trade routes
- goods
- weapons
- tools
- boats / travel
- foodways
- clothing
- household structures
- kinship obligations
- language contact
- French movement
- Native diplomacy
- women’s work
- children’s tasks
- law / church / record systems in later books
- land and political systems across the series

## Rule

Historical systems should enter through lived experience.

Examples:

- a child noticing unfamiliar goods
- women preparing for visitors
- a matriarch interpreting trade implications
- a binding negotiation revealing intertribal politics
- a household object showing outside contact
- a marriage changing power

## Danger To Avoid

Do not pause the story just to explain history.

Make history touch food, bodies, travel, marriage, danger, children, trade, law, and memory.
