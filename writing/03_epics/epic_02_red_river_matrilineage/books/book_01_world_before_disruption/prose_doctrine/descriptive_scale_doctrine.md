# Descriptive Scale Doctrine

Canon status: PROPOSED

## Governing Principle

The prose should feel large, rich, and immersive.

The reader should feel they have entered a complete world: river, weather, food, tools, bodies, trade goods, ceremonies, households, children, kinship, danger, humor, and memory.

## Detail Standard

Descriptions should not be decorative only.

Each detail should reveal at least one of:

- place
- power
- culture
- relationship
- history
- labor
- trade
- gender role
- family structure
- emotional state
- coming rupture
- survival knowledge

## Scale Target

The book should feel epic and deeply inhabited, not fast or thin.

## Danger To Avoid

- encyclopedia dumping
- generic “beautiful nature” language
- museum display description
- detail that does not affect character, power, place, or story
