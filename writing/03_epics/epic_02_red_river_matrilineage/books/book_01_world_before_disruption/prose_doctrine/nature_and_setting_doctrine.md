# Nature and Setting Doctrine

Canon status: PROPOSED

## Purpose

The Red River world must feel magnificent, alive, dangerous, nourishing, and specific.

## Nature Should Function As

- home
- food source
- memory holder
- trade route
- boundary
- danger
- spiritual/cultural landscape
- child-world
- women’s working environment
- political geography
- witness to change

## Required Sensory Fields

Each major setting should eventually track:

- light
- water
- mud / soil / riverbank
- plants
- animals
- insects
- weather
- smell
- sound
- food preparation
- movement through space
- child perception
- women’s labor
- trade objects
- danger signs

## Rule

Setting should never be only backdrop.

Setting is power.
