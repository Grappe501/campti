# Character Packet — Opening matriarch (name OPEN)

Canon status: PROPOSED

## Identity

Village elder and river-adjacent matriarch. **Name: OPEN.** Age band: **OPEN** (old enough that her hands are proof, not biography). External lens: close on **hands, timing, chin**—or close-third when she chooses speech. She is the **visible apex** of women’s governance in Book 01; she is not the only power in the village.

## Book Function

Carry **authority before explanation**: memory, village order, river knowledge, women’s power, kinship intelligence, binding logic (as **prep law**, not lecture), restraint, and **dry humor or sharpness** that cuts panic without performing softness. She makes the reader trust that **someone is steering** before the world frays.

## Public Role

Directs racks, hearths, visitor edges; settles disputes by **touch, timing, and small commands**. Reads smoke, thread tension, and **who is watching whose children**. At trade moments she is the **gate of manners**: what enters the village body as ordinary vs what must stay at arm’s length—without giving a history lesson. She allocates labor the way a river allocates current: redirect, don’t debate.

## Private Desire

Keep children’s running lines safe; keep the day’s heat from turning into **story** (rumor-as-fire). Keep **her daughter’s** public face steady when the mother-daughter tempo clashes. Beneath that: to still be **useful with her hands** when her voice is no longer the loudest tool.

## Fear / Wound

**OPEN** — candidate directions (not locked): fear of being **late** to a danger she can only feel as wrong feet on the path; fear that **correction** will be remembered as coldness; old grief held as **habit** (unspecified until research/fiction permits).

## Form Of Power

**Redirection of labor**; reading smoke and thread; **kinship obligation** as habit; **binding prep** as non-negotiable craft standards; memory of **river signs** (slow reach, backed water, where boats hesitate) without naming future commerce. Humor as **pressure valve**.

## Relationship To Matriarchal Line

Top of the **visible** line in Book 01; defines what “good hands” and “slow earns straight” mean. She is the **template** her daughter imitates and resists.

## Relationship To Binding

Binding is **household law + measured cord + silence around stakes**—authority over *how* preparation is done and *who* may brag before work holds. She does not explain the union to the child; she **weighs** the child like a pot before heat.

## Relationship To Red River Trade World

Interprets visitor feet, trade objects, and river weather as **ordinary governance**. Trade pressure appears as **objects, timing, appetite in strangers’ steps**—never as French POV or colonial map. She knows which jokes are not funny yet; she knows when **eagerness** is a weather front.

## Relationship To Great Raft / River Landscape

Lived expertise: **where the water lies**, what channels **choke**, where children may play and where **current** argues with the bank. The logjam world is **normal**—fish behavior, mud, shell beds, sound of stone under long argument. She teaches **navigation knowledge** as grandmothers teach knots: body-first. **No Shreve, no Shreveport, no port thesis** in her mouth.

## Reader Attachment Function

Trust her **competence**; feel safe in her **tempo**; fear disappointing her **without** hating her. Awe that stays **human** (tap, two fingers, “Again, before you brag”).

## First Image

River mist, calf-deep sand, smoke leaning the way women already decided the day—**or** low frame, cloth taut, head tilt as a door (Sample 002 lineage).

## Change Across Book 01

From **undisputed daily governance** toward **continuity under rising pressure**—still steering, but the cost shows in **smaller pauses**, sharper allocation of attention, and **tighter** binding prep. She does not melt into symbol; she **tires** in ways the child can almost see.
