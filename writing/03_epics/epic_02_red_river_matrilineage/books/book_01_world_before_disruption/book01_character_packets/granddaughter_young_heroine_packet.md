# Character Packet — Granddaughter / young heroine (name OPEN)

Canon status: PROPOSED

## Identity

Pre-raid child in matriarchal household (exact name **OPEN**). POV-capable: close child lens preferred for her chapters.

## Book Function

Reader must love her as a **full child** — pride, wrongness, appetite for place — before binding/rupture become plot engines.

## Public Role

Learns knots; runs messages; competes with cousins; tests boundaries around women’s quiet work.

## Private Desire

**Place**: to be measured against something that matters, not only longer legs.

## Fear / Wound

Fear of being laughed at; fear of being **unimportant** in the women’s quiet; ordinary shame of failed loops.

## Form Of Power

Observation; imitation; small disobediences that still return to obedience; pocket-objects and tactile memory.

## Relationship To Matriarchal Line

Watched and weighed “like a pot before heat”; taught by hands, not speeches.

## Relationship To Binding

Half-understands binding year as **height / real job / prize-language corrected** — must not read as wedding fantasy.

## Relationship To Red River Trade World

Visitors mean sugar twists and jokes not funny yet; she reads eagerness as weather.

## Reader Attachment Function

Protect her **dignity** without sanctifying her; let her be clever, wrong, stubborn, tender.

## First Image

Cord on a peg too high for pride alone; shell in pocket; listening to hands.

## Change Across Book 01

## What Survives

Physical literacy and emotional map of being watched with love.
