## Identity

Adult daughter of the opening matriarch; mother of the granddaughter. **Name: OPEN.** She is the **second tempo** of the house—the one whose wrists catch jars, whose voice translates grandmother’s law into **steps a child can reach**. She must **occupy the center of the braid** often enough that Book 01 never reads as “grandmother, then child, with a shadow between.”

## Book Function

**Bridge that carries weight:** inherited power transformed into **household permanence** (storeroom, racks, cord discipline, public face at the visitor edge). **Mothering as political work**: who runs where, whose shame is corrected in public, which child is watched when smoke hours tighten. She carries **marriage/binding consequence** as fatigue and precision—not as bridal fantasy.

## Public Role

Carries bundles; corrects children mid-path; speaks **trade-facing** lines when the household needs a younger mouth at the edge. Executes binding-year prep: measuring, coiling, marking—**adult-sized stakes** kept out of the child’s ear until the child is already listening. She is the face that **softens** when the child does well and **does not perform** softness when the village is watching.

## Private Desire

Finish unfamiliar work before it becomes a **story**; be seen as **competent** by her mother **and** **useful** to her children; secure a **future that holds** (binding, food, reputation) without admitting how much she is gambling on **rhythm** holding.

## Fear / Wound

Caught between **mother’s tempo** and **children’s speed**; fear of **mouth running ahead of racks**; fear that **correction** will be read as coldness by her daughter; **OPEN** deeper wound (shame event, loss, rivalry—TBD) that must not collapse into exposition.

## Form Of Power

**Correction-as-care**; translation of matriarch rules into **reachable steps**; **public dignity** under visitor eyes; **measuring discipline** as love language (scrap marker on coil—Sample 002 lineage). She allocates **attention**—who gets taught now, who waits.

## Relationship To Matriarchal Line

Inherits authority **gradually and conflictually**: she can be **checked in front of kin** (CAND-011 energy) and still be the **executor** the village relies on. She is not a lesser copy—she is **permanence mechanics** to the matriarch’s **governance pattern**.

## Relationship To Binding

She **runs** binding prep while keeping stakes **adult-sized**; she refuses princess language (“not a prize—it’s a knot that tightens slowly”). Her body carries **consequence**: wrist catches, coil markers, measured cord out of reach. Tension: binding as **security** vs binding as **narrowing**—felt, not debated in thesis.

## Relationship To Red River Trade World

**First interface** with visitor eagerness; knows which jokes are not funny yet; knows when to **shorten** speech at the path. Trade is **feet, cloth twists, sugar rumors**—never French-centered plot. She reads pressure as **weather** the household must dress for.
