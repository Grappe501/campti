# Character Packet — Village ensemble (supporting women + Native male interfaces)

Canon status: PROPOSED

## Identity

Distributed **Native** village body: women at racks, hearths, dye, pottery taps, frames, storeroom traffic; **male interfaces** as needed (haulers, hunters, boat work, path watchers)—**roles first**, names **OPEN**. Not a chorus that explains theme; a **machine of hands** that makes matriarchal power **plausible** as collective habit.

## Book Function

Prevents lone-heroine syndrome; carries **systems**—who swaps shifts, who repeats a joke wrong, who side-eyes whose child. Supplies **texture** for Sample 002–style beats: laughter “not quite” ridicule, clatter like pottery, baby fuss stopped by unseen hip.

## Public Role

Labor noise; quick help; peer correction; jokes that aren’t funny yet; **visitor moments** as ensemble tension (feet, eyes, bundles)—always **Native-centered** observation.

## Private Desire

Default: **finish the day without breaking rhythm**; protect own children’s faces; avoid being the one whose mouth runs ahead of smoke.

## Fear / Wound

Scarcity; stranger-fish; ridicule; being the weak link in prep chain.

## Form Of Power

Collective habit; peer correction; shift swaps; **distributed watching** of children.

## Relationship To Matriarchal Line

They **perform** the matriarch’s tempo when she is elsewhere; they also **negotiate** small resistances—real society.

## Relationship To Binding

Background prep; whispers; measuring tasks passed hand-to-hand; **different households** rumor different binding “facts” for child echo.

## Relationship To Red River Trade World

Ordinary exchange surfaces; trade objects as daily fact—**never** French political exposition in ensemble mouth.

## Relationship To Great Raft / River Landscape

Shared child-boundary rules; boat hesitation; **mud and bank** as communal workplace; seasonal **passable / not passable** talk as logistics, not history lesson.
