# Daughter Viewpoint Plan

Canon status: PROPOSED

**HI-004:** Terminal child experience is the **matriarch’s granddaughter** (young heroine). Use **daughter** only for **mother–daughter** (middle generation) relationships. See `../human_inputs/HI-004_matriarchal_three_generation_story_engine.md`, `../core_storyline/emotional_plot_daughter_belonging.md`.

## Purpose

The final raid must be experienced through the **granddaughter’s** terror, confusion, sensory perception, and limited understanding (HI-001).

## Granddaughter Before the Ending

- **PROPOSED (SRC-DUAL-002):** Age ~11–12, small-framed — stamina and sightlines differ from adults; emotional volatility is plausible without “adult plotting.”
- **PROPOSED (SRC-DUAL-005):** She is the **child focal figure** in the two-hand spine: born into a system that mostly “holds” for her; **edge-of-change** must read as **child-scale** misreads, not prophecy.
- **OPEN:** Who carries most on-page POV before Act IV — decision pending (`D-004`).

## What She Loves

- **PROPOSED:** People first: **matriarch’s** lap-voice, peer laughter, a named dish, a river game, a favorite tool too heavy for her, an elder’s story that repeats.
- **PROPOSED:** Places: specific bend, smoke smell, dogs, hammocks, the sound of the community working together.
- **OPEN:** Sacred vs ordinary love — how she names “sacred” without theology lecture.

## What She Notices

- **PROPOSED (SRC-DUAL-004):** **Bodies and tasks**: hands, tone shifts, who avoids whose eyes, who gets the extra portion, who is tired.
- **PROPOSED:** **Sensory hierarchy**: weather on skin, river height, insects, firelight, footpaths — useful for terminal overload later.
- **PROPOSED:** **Pattern breaks** as social oddity before she names them as politics: a stranger’s gear, a new word, adults stopping talk when she enters.

## What She Misunderstands

- **PROPOSED:** Adult negotiations (trade, alliance, obligation transfers) as “grown-up noise” or as personal moods.
- **PROPOSED:** Binding as **pageant + happiness** more than contract — partial truth, not lie to reader if adults also feel joy.
- **OPEN:** Which misunderstandings become **retroactively** legible after raid without feeling like retroactive cheat.

## What Adults Keep From Her

- **PROPOSED (SRC-DUAL-001):** Anything that would read as **thriller briefing** — she does not get the map of French intentions.
- **PROPOSED:** Protective silence, euphemism, changed subject — must still respect `SRC-SPINE-002` (no modern therapy diction; show through behavior).
- **OPEN:** How much is protective vs simply **not her business** in culture — needs social-structure read.

## What the Wedding / Binding Means to Her

- **PROPOSED:** “We are the kind of people who **keep going**” — joy as continuity proof.
- **PROPOSED:** Personal stakes: new kin role (helper, cousin-by-marriage OPEN), new clothes, embarrassment, pride, belonging in public.
- **OPEN:** Whether she expects a concrete change in **her** daily work/obligations after binding.

## What the Raid Looks Like Through Her

- **CONFIRMED (HI-001 / human direction):** World-collapse sensation; she does not understand; **French soldier carries her away** — final image.
- **PROPOSED:** Perception modes: sound stacking, faces disappearing, wrong silence, wrong heat, wrong order of events, inability to locate **matriarch’s or mother’s** voice in chaos (**HI-004**).
- **NOT ALLOWED (HI-001 + HI-002):** Battle map, strategic clarity, clean causation — see `terminal_raid_knowledge_firewall.md`.

## What She Cannot Understand During the Raid

- **PROPOSED:** Why **she** is spared; who decides; what French words mean; whether this is punishment; whether everyone is dead; what happens next.
- **OPEN:** Whether she misidentifies uniforms, flags, or individuals — only if sensory-grounded.

## What the Reader Must Not Be Told

- **CONFIRMED (HI-001 / HI-002):** Full political/historical explanation in-scene; aftermath beyond carry-away; omniscient “why this raid now.”
- **PROPOSED:** Any sentence that comforts the reader with **comprehension** the child does not have in the moment.

## Open Questions

- **OPEN:** Single POV vs tight third with rare slips — craft lock.
- **OPEN:** How much French dialogue (if any) she hears as pure sound.
- **OPEN:** Dream/imagery habits before ending — risk of seeming to foreshadow if too symbolic.
