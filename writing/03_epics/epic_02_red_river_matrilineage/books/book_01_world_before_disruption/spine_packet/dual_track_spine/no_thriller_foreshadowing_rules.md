# No-Thriller Foreshadowing Rules

Canon status: PROPOSED

## Pass linkage

- **PROPOSED:** Cross-check every Act I–III beat against `fill_pass_01/extracted_hidden_pressure_material.md` and `joy_before_rupture_audit.md` — if a scene’s *primary* payload is “danger incoming,” revise.

## Core Rule

The book should not train the reader to wait for the raid.

## Allowed

- background unease
- small tensions
- distant political/weather/trade/social disturbances
- adult conversations the daughter cannot interpret
- details that become meaningful only after the raid

## Not Allowed

- obvious massacre setup
- villain-forward suspense
- battle countdowns
- repeated warnings
- scenes whose main purpose is to announce danger
- external narrator explaining the coming attack

## Retroactive Coherence Rule

After the ending, the reader may look back and see that the world was more fragile than they understood.

Before the ending, the reader should mainly feel attached to life continuing.

## Practical test (PROPOSED)

- If a chapter ending makes the reader **excited to see the raid**, it fails.
- If a chapter ending makes the reader **want the binding to succeed**, it passes.
