# HI-002 — Wedding / Binding False Ending

## Human Direction Summary

Book 01 should appear to be moving toward a joyful wedding or binding.

The reader should believe the emotional ending of the book is going to be continuity, love, family, and “forever after.”

The community should feel alive, beautiful, funny, intimate, and worth loving.

The reader should intentionally fall in love with multiple characters across the book.

The ending raid should feel as sudden and incomprehensible to the reader as it does to the people in the encampment.

The attack should not feel like a battle sequence. It should feel like a child’s world collapsing.

The raid is based on an actual historical attack involving an actual person, but it will be woven into the fictional story world.

The final attack must be experienced from the daughter’s viewpoint.

The daughter is approximately 11 or 12, small-framed, terrified, and unable to understand the full context of what is happening.

The ending image remains:

A French soldier grabs her, puts her over his shoulder, and carries her away from the destroyed encampment.

## Visible Story Promise

The visible story promise should be:

- family continuity
- wedding / binding
- future lineage
- community celebration
- life continuing as it has for generations
- beloved characters moving toward joy

## Hidden Story Function

The hidden story function is:

- historical rupture
- collapse of continuity
- severing of daughter from matriarch/community
- violence entering a world that did not emotionally expect it
- the reader feeling loss because the people were made fully alive first

## Reader Experience Rule

The reader should think the ending is moving toward a wedding/binding conclusion until it does not.

The shock should come from the collapse of expectation.

Do not structure the book so the reader spends the entire time waiting for the raid.

## Foreshadowing Rule

Foreshadowing may exist only as retroactive coherence.

Allowed:

- small tensions
- distant unease
- misunderstood signs
- adult conversations the child does not fully grasp
- historical pressure in the background
- fragile hints that only make sense after the ending

Not allowed:

- obvious massacre foreshadowing
- villain-forward suspense
- battle countdown energy
- repeated warnings that make the reader anticipate the raid
- turning the book into a thriller

## Ending Point of View Rule

The attack must be from the daughter’s viewpoint.

The reader should feel:

- terror
- confusion
- sensory overload
- incomprehension
- the collapse of known faces, known sounds, known places
- the impossibility of understanding why she is spared

The reader should not receive:

- strategic explanation
- adult political analysis
- full military context
- aftermath summary
- external narrator explanation

## Historical Verification Rule

Because this is tied to an actual historical attack and actual person, later verification is required before canon promotion.

The project must identify:

- the historical attack
- the actual person being adapted
- the date/date range
- involved French force or faction
- involved Natchitoches people/community
- documented facts
- uncertain or contested facts
- where fiction begins
- what names/terms are historically appropriate
- what must be handled with care
