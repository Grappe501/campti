## Human Direction Summary

Book 01 should be structured in three major thirds.

Each third focuses on a new matriarchal figure and how she takes her role, builds permanence, strengthens family power, and carries leadership forward.

The raid happens in the third major section, to the granddaughter of the opening matriarch.

The granddaughter is the future of the line and the future of the leadership structure.

She is connected to the strongest leadership bloodline in the story world.

Her father is the tribe’s male leader and hero.

Her mother married King Campti. This is an intentional historical stretch and must be tracked as PROPOSED pending verification.

The granddaughter is, for story purposes, treated as the tribe’s future — almost like a princess, though manuscript terminology should be culturally careful.

She is pledged to marry the strongest male leader in the tribe.

Her marriage/binding is meant to continue Red River leadership, bind peace, strengthen kinship, and balance relationships among tribes.

Her mother and mother’s mother also married heirs or leaders from other tribal groups to bind peace, kinship, and intertribal balance.

The book should show how women create and preserve political, household, kinship, and spiritual continuity through binding across generations.

## Book Structure

### Third I — Opening Matriarch

The opening matriarch is the original center of power.

She leads her community, holds memory, manages kinship, understands trade and danger, and sets the pattern for women’s leadership.

Her story shows the origin of the matriarchal strength that carries through the book.

### Third II — Daughter / Mother Generation

The daughter/mother generation receives and transforms the matriarchal role.

This generation strengthens family permanence through marriage/binding, alliance, household leadership, motherhood, and political intelligence.

This includes the marriage to King Campti as a PROPOSED / fictionalized-history thread pending verification.

### Third III — Granddaughter / Young Heroine

The granddaughter shows the most promise.

She is the child of leadership, the future of the people, and the emotional center of the wedding/binding false ending.

She is being prepared for a binding that appears to secure the future.

The reader should believe her future represents continuity, balance, and Red River leadership.

The raid destroys this expected future and carries her away.

## Governing Story Principle

The book is about women carrying power across generations.

The men may be historically visible, but the women are the true structural centers of the novel.

## Internal Language Rule

“Princess” may be useful internal shorthand, but manuscript language should be culturally careful.

Potential manuscript-facing language:

- promised daughter
- future matriarch
- child of leadership
- binding heir
- granddaughter of the matriarch
- girl everyone sees as tomorrow
- one through whom peace is meant to continue

## King Campti Rule

King Campti / Campti as male leader should be tracked as:

PROPOSED — intentional historical stretch pending verification.

Do not treat this as confirmed history without a source trail.

Do not let the European word “king” distort Native leadership structures unless the story is intentionally showing a French label or outsider misunderstanding.
