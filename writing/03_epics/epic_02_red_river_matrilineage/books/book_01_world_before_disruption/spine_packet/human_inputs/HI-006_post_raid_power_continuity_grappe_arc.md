## Human Direction Summary

The raid does not stop the line or the power.

The raid changes where and how the women exercise power.

The young heroine / Marie Therese figure survives, eventually marries Jacques Guedon, and begins building influence inside the French colonial system while maintaining connections with Red River Native communities.

Her daughter, Marie Louise Marguerite Guedon, becomes the next major matriarchal continuity figure.

Marie Louise Marguerite is promised/married to a first husband, then widowed.

That first husband must be researched and built into the story.

After Alexis Grappe arrives and becomes part of the fort / Red River / trade-route world, Marie Louise Marguerite is finally matched with him.

She and Alexis move into the great north bend / Caddo / upper Red River trade world, raise children, and help solidify the northern trade-route stronghold.

Their son becomes a major historical carrier for Books 4 and 5.

## Power Continuity Rule

Do not treat the raid as the end of Native power.

Treat the raid as a rupture that forces matriarchal power to adapt.

The women continue building:

- family power
- land power
- trade power
- kinship power
- French-system fluency
- Red River tribal connection
- children and descendants
- cultural brokerage
- long-term survival

## Book 01 Ending Rule

Book 01 still ends with the young heroine carried away.

The reader should not yet receive the full explanation of what she becomes.

But the system must know:

she is not erased.
