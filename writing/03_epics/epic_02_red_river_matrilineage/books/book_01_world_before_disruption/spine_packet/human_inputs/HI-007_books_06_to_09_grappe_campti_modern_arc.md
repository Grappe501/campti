## Human Direction Summary

The epic continues beyond Books 4 and 5.

Books 6 and 7 carry the Grappe / Campti story into the years leading to and through the Civil War and Reconstruction.

The story then transitions into the 20th century and centers Book 8 on Buford Isadore Grappe and his wife Bobby / Barbara.

Buford and Bobby lived through the Depression, raised 13 surviving children, and reshaped Campti through the force of their family persona.

Buford was a one-armed city marshal.

He lost one arm at age 18 in a hunting accident and was shot again in his 40s while turkey hunting.

Their 13 surviving children create a strong transition from Book 8 into Book 9.

Book 9 carries the story to current Arkansas and Steve Grappe and his wife Kelly, including Kelly Grappe’s run for Arkansas Secretary of State.

The epic ends with the family line in modern Arkansas public life.

## Governing Series Principle

The family line does not merely survive.

It repeatedly adapts to new systems of power:

- Native trade and kinship systems
- French colonial systems
- Red River / Caddo / Natchitoches trade systems
- Civil War and Reconstruction systems
- Depression-era survival
- Campti civic authority
- modern Arkansas politics and public service

## Women’s Historical Arc Rule

The women are not background support.
