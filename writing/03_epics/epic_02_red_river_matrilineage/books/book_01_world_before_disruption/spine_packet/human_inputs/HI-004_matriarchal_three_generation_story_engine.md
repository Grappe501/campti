## Human Direction Summary

The raid happens to the **granddaughter** of the **opening matriarch**.

The opening matriarch is the **lead of her community**.

The wedding/binding trajectory **centers the granddaughter’s future** and the community’s hope for continuity.

The story crosses three generations and should solidify matriarchal leadership, binding, kinship, and what carries the **Grappe** line through the centuries.

The novel should be told **primarily through women’s points of view** and **women’s lived experience**.

Men may be historical narrative centers in records, but **women** are the emotional, cultural, household, and continuity centers of the story.

## Story Rule

Build the Native world through **women’s lives**.

The women should not exist only as victims of the raid. They should be leaders, memory holders, organizers, mothers, grandmothers, daughters, girls, rivals, teachers, negotiators, food-makers, interpreters, protectors, and carriers of continuity.

## Core Story Correction

Book 01 should not be framed merely as a girl surviving a raid.

Book 01 is about a **matriarchal world** preparing to carry itself forward through the **granddaughter’s binding**, before historical violence ruptures that future.

## Terminology

Prefer **granddaughter** / **young heroine** / **matriarch’s granddaughter** / **young binding figure** for the focal child. Use **daughter** only when naming a specific **mother–daughter** relationship (middle generation).
