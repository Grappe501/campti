## The Question

What may Book 1 **show** at the ending about **outside/colonial horizon** (including anchors like **1690 / 1714** in `SRC-SPINE-001`), while preserving the **pre-disruption interior** contract described in `SRC-SPINE-005`?

## Why This Matters

This is the clearest “climax vs doorway” lever: wrong choice collapses Book 1 into **external plot** or confuses reader about what is historically witnessed vs inferred.

## Option A

### Description

**Strict interior ending:** ending contains **no** direct depiction of French post / Tonti-like expeditions as on-stage set-pieces; outside change appears only as **local symptoms** (rumor, trade oddness, witness gaps).

### Strengths

Strongest alignment with “barely touches” intent (`SRC-SPINE-005`).

### Risks

Some readers may want clearer “history is arriving” signal.

### What this option makes possible

A disciplined Book 1→epic handoff without colonial spectacle.

### What this option blocks or delays

On-stage colonial milestones as dramatic peaks in Book 1.

## Option B

### Description

**Interior ending + epistemic horizon:** same as A, plus an explicit **reader-facing** horizon beat that is clearly **not** a full scene of the colonial event (e.g., documentary frame, song, trade tally, genealogy memory) — **only** if labeled and reviewed.

### Strengths

Can acknowledge anchors without staging them.

### Risks

Easy to slide into faux-doc exposition if not tightly crafted (later risk, not this pass).

### What this option makes possible

Satisfying “history pressure” without climax theft.

### What this option blocks or delays

Direct dramatization of 1690/1714 as centerpiece.

## Option C, if needed

### Description

**On-stage horizon beat (high risk):** a late Act IV scene includes **direct encounter** with an external expedition/post precursor — only with explicit sourcing + sensitivity review.

### Strengths

Maximum historical dramatization.

### Risks

Highest chance of violating Book 1 interior contract and guardrails (`TN-002`).

### What this option makes possible

A “big” historical set-piece ending.

### What this option blocks or delays

A clean “system-first” Book 1 identity.

## Recommended Provisional Choice
