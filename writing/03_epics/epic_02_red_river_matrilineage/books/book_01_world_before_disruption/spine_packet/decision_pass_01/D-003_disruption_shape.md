## HI-002 Refinement

The disruption should not dominate the visible plot.

The visible plot should move toward wedding/binding, communal continuity, and emotional attachment.

The disruption shape is:

Hidden pressure / distant historical instability beneath visible joy, culminating in sudden catastrophic shock.

The reader should not feel they are waiting for the attack. The attack should arrive like the collapse of a beloved world.

## The Question

What is Book 1’s **primary disruption grammar**: slow systemic drift, a sharp demographic/system shock, or an intentional braid — and if shock is used, **where** does it land?

## Why This Matters

Disruption shape drives **pacing**, **scene protocol** (“subtle wrongness” vs catastrophe), and whether the book honors the **non-villain-external** guardrail emphasis in existing notes (`SRC-SPINE-002`, `SRC-SPINE-005`).

## Option A

### Description

**Drift-first:** disruption is primarily **accumulating pressures** (signals, obligations, margins) until a **social/revelation** turn (not necessarily demographic catastrophe).

### Strengths

Best match to `08` phased engine and story-system scene protocol.

### Risks

Can feel “low motion” if turns aren’t designed sharply enough.

### What this option makes possible

A clean “system story” without importing a shock beat prematurely.

### What this option blocks or delays

High-drama demographic peaks unless added later as optional braid.

## Option B

### Description

**Shock-primary:** a **sourced** demographic / disease / population shock is the defining Book 1 turn (late Act III / IV).

### Strengths

Strong emotional torque; can match certain `chunk9` scaffolding if approved.

### Risks

Sourcing + timeline harmonization burden; can **steal** thematic center from systemic braid if mishandled.

### What this option makes possible

A memorable “fracture” chapter cluster.

### What this option blocks or delays

Simple drift-only architecture; may compress setup.

## Option C, if needed

### Description

**Braid:** **drift-primary** through Acts I–II; optional **sourced shock** as a **late** Act III beat that lands harder because redundancy was already eaten by drift.

### Strengths

Uses both evidence lines without collapsing them.

### Risks

Requires careful orchestration to avoid whiplash (`TN-003`).

### What this option makes possible

Escalation curve with both texture and turn.

### What this option blocks or delays

“Shock-first” cold open unless explicitly chosen.

## Recommended Provisional Choice

**HI-001 variant — Drift-to-terminal-shock:** unease / misread signs / instability through early-mid book; **catastrophic raid** as the ending rupture (sourcing + verification still required; **PROPOSED**).

## Reason for Recommendation

Human direction **HI-001** requires a **late, sudden collapse** while preserving earlier **pressure accumulation** as the believable runway into catastrophe.
