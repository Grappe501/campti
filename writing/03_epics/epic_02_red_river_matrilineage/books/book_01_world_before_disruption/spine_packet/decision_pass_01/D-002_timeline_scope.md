# D-002 — Timeline scope

**Status:** OPEN  
**Canon status:** PROPOSED

**Tension:** `TN-001`  
**Prior log row:** `open_decisions.md` → D-002 (timeline band + ending date)

## The Question

What is the **authoritative in-world time envelope** for Book 1’s on-page story (opening through ending), given:

- `01_WORLD_STATE_1650.md` specifies an **operating band (1648–1655)** and a **Chapter 1 seasonal window** (`SRC-SPINE-003`), and  
- `docs/book1/chunk8.txt` / `chunk9.txt` use **broader mid/late 1600s** scaffolding (including census-era framing) (`SRC-SPINE-005`, `SRC-SPINE-006`)?

## Why This Matters

Timeline choice controls **seasonal obligations**, **who can plausibly witness what**, and whether manifest beats imply **impossible jumps** or wrong ecological signals.

## Evidence From Existing Material

| Source ID | What it suggests | Notes |
|-----------|------------------|-------|
| SRC-SPINE-003 | Strong operational envelope + seasonal cycle for early Book 1 | Best “day-to-day physics” anchor |
| SRC-SPINE-005 | Book 1 may end before outside “really touches” (late-1600s frame referenced in chunk) | Needs reconciliation with envelope |
| SRC-SPINE-006 | Census-aligned generational scaffolding across a wider span | Useful as **research**, dangerous as implicit on-page canon |
| SRC-SPINE-001 | Chronology anchors include 1690 / 1714 | Not automatically Book 1 on-stage |

## Option A

### Description

**Strict envelope:** Book 1 on-page story stays entirely inside **`01_WORLD_STATE_1650.md`** band (1648–1655), including ending.

### Strengths

Maximal alignment with story-system “physics” and Chapter 1 locks; simplest continuity.

### Risks

May **truncate** research-chunk generational arcs unless those chunks are treated as non-binding for Book 1.

### What this option makes possible

Tight seasonal braid; minimal timeline controversy.

### What this option blocks or delays

Large-span census narrative unless deferred to later books or off-page summaries.

## Option B

### Description

**Broad single band:** choose one wider Book 1 span aligned primarily with **`chunk8` / `chunk9`** research scaffolding.

### Strengths

Keeps generational research scaffolding “as-is” for planning.

### Risks

May **fight** `01_WORLD_STATE_1650.md` seasonal specificity unless rewritten or reframed.

### What this option makes possible

Longer runway inside one volume.

### What this option blocks or delays

Clean use of story-system’s explicit seasonal envelope unless merged carefully.

## Option C, if needed

### Description

**Hybrid mapping:** Acts I–III anchored to **`01_WORLD_STATE_1650.md`** operational reality; Act IV “doorway” may use **carefully labeled** epistemic time horizon (rumor, genealogy memory, trade talk) toward later dates **without** claiming unsourced on-stage events.

### Strengths

Preserves **interior fidelity** while allowing research horizons to inform **reader knowledge architecture**.

### Risks

If labeling is sloppy, readers infer on-stage events that are not actually shown.

### What this option makes possible

Bridge to epic without smuggling climax into Book 1.

### What this option blocks or delays

“Easy” use of late anchors as set-piece without explicit human permission.

## Recommended Provisional Choice

**Option C — Hybrid envelope** (see `decision_summary.md`).

## Reason for Recommendation

It keeps the strongest **operational** anchor (`SRC-SPINE-003`) while avoiding forced collapse of the research corpus into a single incompatible year-band.

## Human Decision Needed

Pick **A/B/C** (or a written variant) and specify: **latest on-stage year** allowed, and whether **doorway** may reference later dates **only** as epistemic layers.
