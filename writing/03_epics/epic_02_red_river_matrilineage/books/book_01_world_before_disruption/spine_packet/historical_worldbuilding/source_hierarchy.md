# Source Hierarchy

Canon status: PROPOSED

## Tier 1 — Highest Weight

- primary records
- church records
- colonial correspondence
- archival documents
- tribal historical materials
- archaeological or ethnohistorical scholarship
- peer-reviewed scholarship

## Tier 2 — Useful but Needs Corroboration

- state historic-site materials
- reputable historical encyclopedias
- museum materials
- federal acknowledgment petitions
- specialist genealogical work with citations

## Tier 3 — Leads Only

- unsourced family trees
- social media posts
- general history blogs
- AI summaries
- memory fragments without documentation

## Rule

No fact moves into `writing/01_canon/` until it has a source trail and a canon decision record.

## Multi-book / series claims

Genealogical and biographical links across Books 01–05 (e.g. **HI-006** Guedon–Besson–Grappe thread, **King Campti** in HI-005) remain **PROPOSED** or **OPEN** in spine and `series_arc/` until each claim meets this hierarchy and gets an explicit canon promotion pass — **no** silent upgrade to verified history.
