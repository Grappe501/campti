# Story Promises

Canon status: PROPOSED

**HI-004:** Promises include **women-centered** story engine. **HI-005:** **Three-thirds** matriarchal ascension + **King Campti** only as **PROPOSED** until verified. **HI-006:** The **epic** is not “the line ends at the raid” — power and line **continue** in new forms (**PROPOSED** until documented); Book 01 **still** ends at carry-away without sequel dump.

## Promises To The Reader

- You will enter a living Native world, not a backdrop.
- You will come to love multiple people.
- The wedding/binding will matter for real.
- The **granddaughter** will be a full child before she is a survivor.
- The Red River trade world will shape life without becoming a lecture.
- The ending will not explain away terror.
- The raid will rupture a future the reader believed in.
- The story will continue across generations.
- You will see **matriarchal leadership** and **women’s continuity** as structural, not decorative.
- **This is a women-centered epic.**
- **The reader will understand power through grandmothers, mothers, daughters, households, bindings, children, and kinship.**
- **Men may appear as leaders in history, but women carry the emotional and structural truth of the story.**
- **The raid ends Book 01’s expected future — not the epic’s claim that women’s power can endure through adaptation (HI-006; reader not lectured at B01 close).**
- **Later books trace the same power through marriage, widowhood, trade, land, and brokerage — all PROPOSED until sourced.**

## Promises To Avoid

- This is not a generic massacre story.
- This is not a French-centered frontier story.
- This is not trauma without life.
- This is not a thriller countdown.
- This is not a history lecture wearing a plot.
- Women will not exist **only** as victims of the raid — they are leaders, memory holders, organizers, negotiators, kin, rivals, teachers (**HI-004**).
- **Do not** present **King Campti** material as verified historical fact without evidence (**HI-005**).
