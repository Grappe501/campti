# Central Conflict

Canon status: PROPOSED

**HI-004 / HI-005:** Narrative center = **women’s** lived experience and **three-thirds** matriarchal ascension; men (including **granddaughter’s father** as male tribal leader/hero) matter but do not displace structural center.

## Working Central Conflict (HI-005)

Three generations of Native women are building family permanence, intertribal peace, and Red River leadership through binding, kinship, children, and matriarchal authority while the colonial world pressing into the region begins turning trade and contact into existential danger.

## External Conflict

The Red River world is changing through French trade, movement, alliances, pressure, and violence.

## Internal / Community Conflict

The village must negotiate family obligations, binding arrangements, household tensions, generational expectations, intertribal balance, and the meaning of continuity — especially through **women’s** binding work (**HI-005**).

## Granddaughter’s Conflict

She wants to belong, understand, and grow into the world around her, but the adult world is larger and more dangerous than she can comprehend — and she is cast as **child of leadership** with a **pledged binding** future (**HI-005** — verify plausibility).

## Reader Conflict

The reader is invited to hope for the binding and fear only ordinary human disappointments — until the actual danger arrives.

## Matriarch’s Conflict (HI-004 — OPEN)

She exercises authority and care in a world she cannot fully control; the binding is **her** stake in a future the raid may sever from her **granddaughter**.

## King Campti Thread (PROPOSED)

Mother-generation marriage to **King Campti** — **intentional fictionalized history** pending verification; **not** locked as historical fact (**HI-005**).

## Series arena shift (HI-006)

The raid does **not** end Native or matriarchal power in the **epic** sense; it **ruptures** the Book 01 visible future and **relocates** power into captivity → French-system adaptation → subsequent marriages and trade strongholds (**PROPOSED** until documented). Book 01 **conflict on the page** still peaks at rupture and carry-away; later-book pressures are **not** thriller-spoiled in Book 01.
