# Prose Sample 001 RevA Notes

## What changed from original

- **Removed** essay-like river “two languages” block, “recorded futures,” “small units of yes / not yet / wait,” “Continuities lived in cloth…,” and the **closing thesis pair** about the village in miniature / patience’s tongue.
- **Shortened** interior memory beat; kept mother’s “Slower” correction as **single** line.
- **Added** daughter on page with **disagreement-then-compliance** (smoke timing, rack work vs “first watch”); matriarch redirects to **embodied tasks** instead of abstract authority.
- **Replaced** “freckles” tag with **“humming girl”** habit for the net child.
- **Added** sensory anchors: **cypress bark smell**, cane rot/green shoots, sand shelf, traveler at **visitor post** (ordinary pressure, no doom).
- **Ending** now closes on **scale, knot, river taking scale**—image-led, not moral summary.
- **Original text** preserved in `archive/prose_sample_001_original.md`; working line remains `prose_sample_001_opening_matriarch_first_image.md` (unchanged per instructions).
- **Removed** “**No heroics**” (modern cadence); replaced with **“No one runs the racks alone.”**

## Matriarchal authority improvements

- Power shown through **task routing** (racks before smoke, copper waits, stack higher, chin-point to humming girl).
- **Conflict micro-beat** with daughter models relational power, not solo infallibility.
- **Visitor** present as body in correct place; matriarch **does not** explain him—interpretation stays in procedure (“Good enough for now”).

## Women-centered improvements

- **Three generations** on the path: matriarch, daughter (corrects child, argues rack duty, walks two hearths in step), child messenger, **humming girl** at net.
- Women’s hands own **needle, rack pins, shawl prep** without narrator naming “binding doctrine.”
