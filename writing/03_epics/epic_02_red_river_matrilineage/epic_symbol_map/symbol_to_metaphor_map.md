# Symbol To Metaphor Map

Canon status: PROPOSED

## Rule

A symbol may begin as a practical thing.

It becomes metaphor only after the reader has experienced it as real.

Core rule:

```text
Let the object be real first. Let metaphor arrive later.
```

| Symbol | Practical Use | Emotional Meaning | Historical Meaning | Metaphor It Becomes | Risk If Overused |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sassafras leaves | food / medicine / seasoning | care, healing, teaching | Native plant knowledge | memory that can be ground, carried, and tasted | mystical cliché; flattened tribe |
| Gumbo pot | feeding family | collective survival | blended Louisiana histories | the family itself (only when earned) | over-explaining the metaphor |
| Cord / knot | tying, binding, teaching | continuity / obligation | kinship and marriage logic | what holds after rupture | too frequent; thesis |
| River | travel / trade / food | motion / fate / danger | Red River route | history moving through people | generic river poetry |
| Cloth / shawl | sewing, rank, weather | preparation, dignity | material culture | future you can touch | costume drama |
| Smoke / fire | cook, heat, craft | labor edges | domestic technology | avoid premature “doom” read | doom cliché |
| Shell / mud | play, gathering, bank work | child agency | place ethics | private truth vs public story | prettiness |
| Silence | breath, pause, refusal | shame, protection, power | surveillance / survival | memory system | vagueness |
| Missing arm | limitation, adaptation | temper, pride, pain | public service body | cost of usefulness | spectacle |

## Operator check

Before a symbol “means,” log one **physical** beat a reader can feel in the body or hands.
