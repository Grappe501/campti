# Subplot Misdirection Symbol Rules

Canon status: PROPOSED

## Human direction

Create plot twists mainly in smaller subplots.

These subplots should feel meaningful enough to draw reader attention, while the larger plot quietly plants deeper details in the background.

When the main plot later develops, the reader should feel they sensed something coming but cannot immediately recall how they knew.

## Purpose

Use small subplot twists as attention magnets.

They should:

- entertain
- deepen character
- create emotional investment
- distract from larger structural planting
- make the world feel layered
- hide long-range symbols in plain sight

## Rule

The small subplot must still matter emotionally.

It cannot be fake filler.

## Misdirection pattern

1. Present a smaller story with vivid details.
2. Give it a satisfying twist or reversal.
3. During that attention, plant a larger-symbol or arc detail quietly.
4. Do not point at the larger detail.
5. Let the larger detail return later with new meaning.
