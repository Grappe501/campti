# Chapter opening paragraph doctrine

Canon status: PROPOSED

## Human direction

Every chapter in the entire epic should begin with a major literary pass.

The first paragraph should be longer and more carefully built than a normal chapter opening.

The first paragraph should contain, without announcing itself:

- tone for the chapter
- symbolic seeds
- mood
- pace
- hidden foreshadowing
- emotional pressure
- one or more future echoes
- the chapter’s secret argument
- the chapter’s breath pattern

After reading the chapter, the reader should be able to return to the first paragraph and realize the chapter was already hidden there.

## Rule

The first paragraph should not give away the chapter.

It should become more meaningful after the chapter is read.

No casual openings.
