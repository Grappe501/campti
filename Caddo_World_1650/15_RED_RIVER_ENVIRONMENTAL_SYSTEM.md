# Red River Environmental System (Behavioral World Constraints)

## Design Role

The river is not scenery. It is a governing system that shapes timing, risk, movement, food, and social obligation.

## Core Environmental Truths

- slow and shifting channel behavior
- floodplain and bottomland logic
- humidity and heat as persistent physiological stressors
- insect density affecting labor pacing and rest timing
- natural levee settlement preference with exposure tradeoffs

## Environmental Variables to Track

1. river height trend (falling / stable / rising)
2. bank stability (firm / soft / cut edge)
3. soil moisture (dry / workable / saturated)
4. heat load index (tolerable / heavy / oppressive)
5. insect pressure (low / moderate / high)
6. travel passability (open / delayed / blocked sections)

## Settlement Implications

- storage strategy must account for moisture and flood risk
- labor starts before peak heat and rebalances around weather shifts
- movement choices are constrained by river and ground condition
- field success depends on timing coherence with water behavior

## Flood Logic

Flood is not only destruction; it is also pattern and expectation. Risk comes from timing mismatch.

## Manageable flood
- known adaptation behaviors
- labor and movement re-sequenced

## Bad flood timing
- planting disruption
- store vulnerability
- kin obligation stress due to uneven losses

## Drought/Heat Logic

- reduced yield reliability
- strain on labor endurance
- intensified allocation politics in households

## Animal and Sound Cues

- dog behavior changes can prefigure social and environmental shift
- bird and insect sound pattern changes can signal weather-turn windows
- silence anomalies should be treated as scene-level intelligence

## Writing the Physical Experience

Always include at least two of:
- tactile: mud, fiber, heat-film, stored grain texture
- olfactory: smoke, damp thatch, standing water, food processing
- auditory: pestle rhythm, insect wall, altered water sound
- physiological: sweat load, breathing pace, skin irritation, fatigue management

## Environmental-to-Social Coupling

When environmental stress rises, automatically test:
- ration policy
- labor redistribution
- delayed obligation repayment
- conflict threshold in kin exchanges

## Chapter 1 Constraints

- one notable environmental irregularity early
- one confirming signal by mid-chapter
- one adaptation decision by line leadership
- no catastrophic collapse in opening chapter

## Long-Arc Campti Link

Repeated environmental pressure and adaptation decisions create the practical conditions that later make relocation and reformulation plausible.
