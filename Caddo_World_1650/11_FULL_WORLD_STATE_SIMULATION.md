# Full World-State Simulation (c. 1650 Natchitoches Red River)

## Simulation Purpose

This model defines baseline life-systems before catastrophic rupture so Chapter 1 can portray competence under pressure.

## World-State Axes

Track each axis every scene-day:

1. **River Condition**
   - low / normal / rising / flood-edge / post-flood instability
2. **Food Security**
   - stable / tight / strained / emergency allocation
3. **Kinship Cohesion**
   - aligned / tense / fractured / unresolved debt
4. **Travel Signal Integrity**
   - clear / delayed / contradictory / silence-heavy
5. **Ritual Regularity**
   - regular / delayed / abbreviated / irregular
6. **External Pressure**
   - distant / rumor-near / corridor-visible / direct

## Seasonal Cycle Engine

## Late Cold -> Early Warm
- tool repair, seed sorting, stores audit, household labor planning
- risk: premature depletion of reserves

## Planting Window
- field prep, planting sequence timing, kin labor exchange
- risk: mistimed planting after water shifts

## Growing Season
- weeding, watch cycles, pest and weather vigilance, selective harvest
- risk: flood pulse, heat stress, localized crop failure

## Harvest and Storage
- processing, drying, pottery storage, distribution rules
- risk: moisture damage, rot, vermin, obligation conflict over allocation

## Cold Period
- ration governance, social compression, memory transfer, craft concentration
- risk: social fracture under scarcity stress

## Daily Labor Ledger (Base Day)

- pre-dawn: fire, water, store check, first assessment of weather and soundscape
- early light: child task assignment, field or processing mobilization
- mid-day: exchange, repair, social scanning, rumor quality checks
- late day: food preparation, kin obligation reconciliation, conflict smoothing
- night: memory transfer, future task locking, risk interpretation

## Failure Cascades

## Example Cascade A: Moisture in Storage
1. food quality uncertainty
2. ration tightening
3. kin tension over distribution
4. alliance stress in reciprocal exchange
5. increased travel risk to compensate

## Example Cascade B: Delayed Returning Party
1. uncertainty in trade intelligence
2. obligation schedule disruption
3. speculation spread through kin network
4. precaution labor reallocation
5. social trust strain if silence persists

## Governance Logic

- continuity is upheld through distributed authority, not a single command voice
- practical authority and ceremonial authority can diverge without collapse
- women hold key continuity controls: seed memory, food timing, belonging logic, obligation continuity

## Scene Injection Rules

Each Chapter 1 scene must include:

- 1 labor action tied to survival
- 1 relationship obligation in play
- 1 environmental signal
- 1 unresolved pressure marker

## Prohibited Distortions

- no "empty land" framing
- no "primitive chaos" framing
- no modern bureaucratic language in interior thought
- no immediate apocalypse framing in opening pages

## Campti Hook

The long arc toward Campti begins as repeated continuity decisions under small disruptions, not as a single dramatic announcement.
