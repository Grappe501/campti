# Natchitoches Language Thought Framework (Writing System)

## Objective

Preserve Natchitoches-centered worldview in cognition while presenting readable English output.

## Core Principle

Characters do not think in modern abstract English categories first. They think through relationship, place, action, obligation, and memory pattern.

## Three-Layer Cognition Pipeline

1. **Internal Layer (native structure)**
   - relation-first
   - place-bound
   - action-consequence linked
   - memory-indexed
2. **Concept Mapping Layer**
   - map internal logic to English concepts without flattening meaning
3. **English Output Layer**
   - readable English that preserves the original logic order where possible

## Composition Rules

- prefer concrete event-linked phrasing over abstract emotion labels
- tie fear/worry to food, kin, timing, land, and obligation
- preserve communal framing where appropriate ("our stores," "our line," "their obligation")
- avoid modern psych jargon and policy vocabulary in interiority

## Allowed Translation Drift

- minor smoothing for readability is allowed
- worldview distortion is not allowed

## Example Transformations (Conceptual)

- abstract-English-first (avoid):
  - "I feel anxious about the future."
- worldview-preserving output:
  - "The river has shifted, and our timing no longer matches it."

- abstract-English-first (avoid):
  - "I am afraid of social conflict."
- worldview-preserving output:
  - "If we miss this obligation, that family will close its door to us."

## Kinship and Social Terms Policy

- retain key kin terms where available and appropriate
- provide context through action and response rather than glossary dumping
- avoid fake fluency or invented lexical claims without source grounding

## Dialogue Guidance

- short spoken lines can remain practical and indirect
- social hierarchy appears through address, timing, silence, and who answers whom
- conflict is often signaled by withholding expected acknowledgment

## Silence Semantics

Silence can encode:
- disagreement without rupture
- danger assessment
- respect protocol
- information protection

Writers must treat silence as information, not empty space.

## Red Flags (Do Not Do)

- generic pan-Indigenous diction
- mystical abstractions replacing concrete social logic
- exposition-heavy "culture explaining itself" dialogue
- flattening all women into one emotional register

## Output Validation Checklist

For each key paragraph ask:
- Is this thought anchored in place/action/obligation?
- Could this line come from a modern office novel unchanged? (if yes, revise)
- Does this preserve communal continuity logic?
