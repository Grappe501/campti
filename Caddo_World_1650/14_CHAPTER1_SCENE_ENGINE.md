# Chapter 1 Scene Engine (Beat-by-Beat Architecture)

## Mission

Build a Chapter 1 opening that proves social intelligence before visible rupture.

## Beat Structure (10 Beats)

## Beat 1: Pre-Dawn Recognition
- focal: elder matriarch
- action: routine check reveals subtle irregularity
- output: no explanation, only recognition

## Beat 2: Household Wake and Labor Allocation
- focal: adult daughter
- action: assigns tasks with one quiet change in plan
- output: competence and hierarchy through action

## Beat 3: Child Witness Layer
- focal: child girl
- action: observes adults suppressing discussion
- output: emotional signal of unspoken pressure

## Beat 4: Field/Store Interface
- focal: younger woman
- action: practical work with near-mistake
- output: training under pressure, not humiliation

## Beat 5: Kin Obligation Contact
- focal: daughter + visiting kin
- action: a routine exchange carries subtle strain
- output: social tension enters without overt conflict

## Beat 6: Environmental Confirmation
- focal: elder
- action: secondary signal confirms first anomaly
- output: pressure is real but still containable

## Beat 7: Travel Intelligence Arrival
- focal: mixed
- action: traveler returns with incomplete information
- output: silence and omission are more alarming than content

## Beat 8: Internal Line Decision
- focal: elder + daughter
- action: continuity-first decision made at personal cost
- output: family line duty over individual ease

## Beat 9: Evening Compression
- focal: younger + child
- action: ordinary tasks under changed emotional weather
- output: world still functioning, but tighter

## Beat 10: Closing Hook
- focal: elder
- action: identifies next-day action based on pressure signs
- output: chapter ends with rising system risk, not spectacle

## Required Payload Per Beat

Each beat should include:
- 1 sensory anchor
- 1 labor action
- 1 social obligation cue
- 1 pressure marker

## Pressure Injection Matrix

- low: irregular sound, delayed response, awkward greeting
- medium: ration shift, labor reassignment, unsettled traveler report
- high (for later chapters): unresolved injury, visible scarcity fracture, alliance break

Chapter 1 should remain low-to-medium, with one medium spike late.

## Voice and Pacing Rules

- keep exposition under action, not over it
- prefer compressed dialogue and meaningful gesture
- let repeated tasks reveal systems continuity
- avoid dramatic monologues in early beats

## Integrity Checks Before Draft Lock

- Does opening prove a functioning society?
- Is the matrilineal line visible through decisions?
- Is disturbance introduced as signal, not event?
- Does the final beat compel continuation without spectacle inflation?

## Campti Integration Prompt Set

- What continuity task is threatened in this beat?
- Who absorbs the cost of adaptation?
- What sign was noticed first, and by whom?
- What is spoken, and what remains intentionally unsaid?
