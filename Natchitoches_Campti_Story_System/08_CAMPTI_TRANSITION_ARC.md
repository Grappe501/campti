# CAMPTI TRANSITION ARC (LONG-RANGE STORY ENGINE)

## Arc Premise

Campti does not emerge from a single dramatic rupture.  
It emerges from accumulated continuity decisions under repeated pressure.

## Five-Phase Arc

## Phase 1: Stable Natchitoches Settlement Logic

- established river-linked household systems
- predictable obligation exchange patterns
- continuity held through matrilineal governance

## Phase 2: Layered Pressure Accumulation

- environmental unpredictability increases cost of routine
- trade and travel signals become less reliable
- kin obligations strain under uneven resource outcomes

## Phase 3: Social and Spatial Fracture Threshold

- repeated adaptation begins to fail at old scale
- alliance choices narrow
- household continuity planning begins to include movement options

## Phase 4: Crossing / Relocation Decision

- movement is framed as continuity protection, not abandonment
- crossing creates memory rupture and identity stress
- line authority is tested by uncertainty and loss

## Phase 5: Reformation Into Campti

- social order is rebuilt through remembered obligations
- place-memory is translated into new geography
- continuity survives displacement through retained kinship logic

## Matrilineal Throughline

At every phase ask:

- what is being carried?
- who carries it?
- what is paid to carry it?

The answer should remain centered on women sustaining lineage memory, practical survival, and belonging continuity.

## Scene-Level Bridge Markers

Use these as early foreshadowing in Book 1:

- recurring concern about river-route reliability
- increased value placed on portable memory objects and names
- subtle re-weighting of which obligations can still be met locally
- emerging language of "holding the line" versus "holding place"

## Avoid

- treating movement as random wandering
- framing relocation as only reaction and not strategic adaptation
- reducing Campti formation to outsider-driven plot mechanics

## Arc Test

By the time crossing occurs, the reader should understand:

the people moved because continuity required movement.
