# WORLD STATE - RED RIVER NATCHITOCHES (c. 1650)

## Frame

This is a functioning river civilization under layered stress, not a collapse narrative.  
Chapter 1 captures order under pressure.

## Temporal Envelope

- operating year-band: 1648-1655
- Chapter 1 active window: late summer into early fall
- memory horizon: elder memory + lived household memory + recent travel intelligence

## Seasonal Operating Cycle

## Spring (Water Authority)

- flood behavior redraws practical boundaries
- planting sequence establishes future social leverage
- labor exchanges encode trust and debt

## Summer (Heat Compression)

- heat, humidity, and insects reduce margin for error
- food growth masks storage vulnerability
- fatigue alters social tone and conflict threshold

## Fall (Accountability Season)

- harvest and storage quality define winter survivability
- each stored resource carries kin-obligation implications
- unresolved obligations become political fault lines

## Winter (Dependency Season)

- movement narrows and social interdependence tightens
- ration logic tests legitimacy of household leadership
- ritual continuity becomes social stabilizer

## Primary Pressure Systems

1. **Environmental Instability**
   - river timing mismatch
   - weather variance
   - pests and spoilage
2. **Food Security**
   - storage risk
   - labor timing error
   - unequal access stress
3. **Kinship Obligation**
   - delayed reciprocity
   - marriage-linked tension
   - witness-based trust erosion
4. **External Unknowns**
   - shifting inter-polity dynamics
   - rumors of outsider influence
   - disease pathways not yet understood

## Knowledge Boundaries

## Known

- land and river patterns
- obligation networks
- household continuity rules

## Unknown But Felt

- true scale of distant expansion pressures
- delayed consequences from altered trade contact
- full scope of coming population shocks

## World-State Tracking Matrix (Per Scene)

- river condition: stable / rising / unstable bank
- food condition: stable / tight / strain
- social condition: aligned / tense / brittle
- signal condition: clear / contradictory / withheld

## Non-Negotiable Scene Law

Every scene must include:

1. one material condition in motion
2. one social consequence attached to it
3. one pressure felt before fully named
