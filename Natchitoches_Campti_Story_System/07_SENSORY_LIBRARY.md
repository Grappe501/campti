# SENSORY LIBRARY (RED RIVER SCENE PALETTE)

## Use Law

Sensory details must drive interpretation, decision, or social consequence.  
Do not deploy as ornamental nature writing.

## Sound Layer

- insect bedtone as baseline continuity marker
- water drag against roots and bank edges
- tool contact sounds (wood, clay, fiber)
- voice compression under social pressure
- abrupt silence as high-value warning signal

## Smell Layer

- wet earth under rising heat
- smoke density shifts by wind direction
- stored grain, clay, and body heat in enclosed spaces
- plant decay in low-water zones
- damp thatch and river air mixing indoors

## Touch Layer

- humidity load on skin and fabric
- mud suction and uneven footing
- roughness of fiber, cane, and tool grips
- retained heat in clay, wood, and packed ground

## Visual Layer

- filtered low-angle light through dense growth
- shadow pockets that hide movement cues
- water sheen/turbidity indicating upstream change
- posture and object angle as social hierarchy signals

## Physiological Layer

- sweat accumulation and grip slippage
- insect irritation affecting concentration
- heat-fatigue impact on speech and judgment speed
- breathing shifts before visible conflict

## Quick Scene Inserts

Use one from each row when drafting:

- sound: interrupted insect wall
- smell: smoke turning against expected wind
- touch: basket handle damp and slick
- visual: someone not standing where they should be

## Constraint

Every sensory note must answer: "What does this change for the character right now?"
