# SOCIAL STRUCTURE - NATCHITOCHES MATRI-KIN ORDER

## Core Organizing Logic

- maternal-line continuity anchors belonging and legitimacy
- obligation is relational, witnessed, and cumulative
- household continuity is social infrastructure, not private preference

## Matrilineal / Often Matrilocal Expression

- kin identity and social placement track through maternal continuity
- marriage reorganizes practical obligations around women's household lines
- authority is distributed across memory, labor, ritual, and diplomacy roles

## Functional Authority Distribution

## Women (Continuity Governance)

- seed memory and food timing
- household continuity and child belonging logic
- obligation tracking across kin network
- conflict smoothing through precedent and practical allocation

## Men (External Interface Roles)

- hunting, route intelligence, and long-distance contact tasks
- diplomatic and security-facing responsibilities
- ceremonial and political representation in some domains

## Governance Without Bureaucratic Institutions

- rank reads through sequence, handling rights, witness behavior, and deference timing
- conflict appears first as etiquette strain, then as explicit dispute
- correction often occurs through role pressure and withheld reciprocity

## Social Debt and Reciprocity

- every exchange records obligation memory
- delayed repayment has reputational consequences
- continuity leadership requires balancing fairness with survival urgency

## Child-Rearing as Continuity Work

- children learn by participation, observation, correction, and silence
- belonging is enacted through tasks and kin response patterns
- failure to transfer social grammar is a structural risk

## Scene Construction Requirements

In each scene, show at least one of:

- who speaks first versus who waits
- who handles food/storage objects
- who is publicly acknowledged or subtly bypassed
- where social correction happens without overt confrontation
