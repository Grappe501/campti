# ENVIRONMENT SYSTEM - RED RIVER BASIN

## Core Rule

Land and water are active governing forces. They regulate labor, risk, social timing, and narrative pace.

## River Behavior Model

- channel flow appears slow but carries structural power
- seasonal rise/fall redefines routes, work order, and access claims
- minor river changes can trigger major social adjustments

## Terrain Classes and Behavioral Effects

## Natural Levees

- preferred settlement bands
- better drainage and movement stability
- visibility advantage with exposure tradeoff

## Bottomland Forest

- source of wood, forage, game, medicinal material
- also concealment zone, navigation risk, and fatigue multiplier

## Cultivation Patches

- food continuity anchor
- publicly legible indicator of household status and planning discipline

## Climate and Body Pressure

- humidity load reduces endurance and increases spoilage risk
- heat governs labor windows and speech patience
- insects shape sleep quality, mood, and attention

## Environmental Signal Lexicon

- insect/bird silence shift = potential disruption
- water color/turbidity change = upstream disturbance
- altered smoke drift = wind and visibility change
- sudden mud softness = transport delay + injury risk
- unusual dog patterning = social or environmental anomaly

## Environment-to-Social Coupling

When environmental pressure rises, automatically test:

- ration behavior
- labor reallocation
- reciprocity timing
- conflict threshold

## Scene Rendering Rules

1. each scene includes at least one active environmental signal
2. signal must alter a human decision
3. sensory detail must serve consequence, not decoration

## Prohibited Usage

- postcard description detached from action
- generic weather statement without social effect
- cinematic spectacle replacing survival logic
